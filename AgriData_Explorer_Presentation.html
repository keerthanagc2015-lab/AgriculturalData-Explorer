Project Overview

🎯
Objective

Build a data visualization platform integrating crop production, yield, and area data across Indian states & districts to enable data-driven decisions for farmers, policymakers, and researchers.

📦
Dataset — ICRISAT

District-level agricultural data covering 313 districts across India, spanning 51 years (1966–2017), with 16,146 rows and 80 columns covering 20+ crop types.

🛠️
Tech Stack
Python (Pandas, Plotly) MySQL Power BI Jupyter Notebook
📊
Deliverables
  • Cleaned & structured dataset
  • EDA with 15+ interactive charts
  • SQL database + 10 business queries
  • 4-page interactive Power BI dashboard

Data Cleaning Pipeline

1
Handled Missing Values
Replaced NaN with 0 for crop area, production & yield; dropped rows with all-null records
2
Removed Negative Values
Replaced negative crop production/yield entries with NaN then 0 — physically impossible values excluded
3
Standardized Column Names
Stripped whitespace, converted to lowercase with underscores for consistent SQL and Pandas access
4
Unit Consistency Verified
All area values in 1000 ha, production in 1000 tons, yield in kg/ha — confirmed across all 80 columns

Key Findings & Insights

🌾 Rice Production

West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh & Punjab are the top 3 rice-producing states. West Bengal's districts (Rice Belt) show consistently high output. Rice production has grown 3x over 51 years.

📈 Maize Yield Growth

Annual average maize yield rose steadily from 1.0 kg/ha (1966) to 3.6 kg/ha (2017) — a 3.6x improvement driven by irrigation upgrades and improved seed varieties.

🥜 Groundnut Districts (2017)

Junagadh (29.8K tons) leads groundnut production in 2017, followed by Ananthapur (19.1K), Rajkot (17.4K), Jamnagar (17.4K) — all from Gujarat & Andhra Pradesh, indicating regional specialization.

🌾 Wheat Yield Districts

Vidisha (MP) tops wheat yield increase at +2,146 kg/ha over last 5 years, followed by Darjeeling (1,839) and Shajapur (1,684) — indicating improved farming practices in MP.

🫒 Oilseed Growth States

Andhra Pradesh (+0.45) and Haryana (+0.22) show highest oilseed production growth. Assam, Gujarat, Chhattisgarh show negative growth (−1.0) indicating sharp cultivation decline.

🔗 Area–Production Correlation

Strong positive correlation confirmed between cultivated area and production for Rice, Wheat & Maize. However, district-level scatter variations indicate differences in yield efficiency & irrigation access.
